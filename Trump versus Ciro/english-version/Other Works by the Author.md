# **Other Works by the Author**

The world functions because it has limits.

But what happens when they disappear?

What begins as progress can turn into a threat.
As time accumulates without end, decisions lose weight, systems become saturated, and balance begins to erode.

These works explore, from different perspectives, the same fundamental question:
what happens when the limits that sustain reality cease to exist?

---

## **The Devil’s Throats: The Invisible System That Sustains—and Threatens—the Global Economy**

An analysis of the critical points where the global economy concentrates and becomes vulnerable.

The Strait of Hormuz is not an exception, but one of several chokepoints—true strategic “throats”—where a local disruption can trigger global consequences.

This book reveals how these largely invisible points sustain the functioning of the world…
and how their fragility can alter everything.

---

## **The Need for the New: The Risk of a World Where No One Dies**

An analytical work that examines how the absence of limits alters the stability of human systems, identity, and continuity.

---

## **The Need for the New: Why Immortality Could Collapse Human Existence**

A narrative approach that transforms the concept into experience, exploring the consequences of a world without an end.

---

## **Lethal Eternity: The First Limit**

A direct and impactful novel that shows how the removal of the most fundamental limit of existence triggers a progressive process of destabilization.

---

These works form a single conceptual universe, developed through analysis, narrative, and confrontation.

Available in Spanish and English.
